Helen Keller


A strong woman who fought for many people

"The best and most beautiful things in the world cannot be seen or even touched - they must be felt with the heart."

-Helen Keller, Brainy Quote

Here is a timeline of Helen Keller's life:

  • 1880-Born in Tuscumbia, Alabama.
  • 1882- Contracts scarlet fever or meningitis and becomes blind and deaf. She struggles to communicate with others.
  • 1887- Meets Anne Sulliva and becomes her student. She learns using hand gestures and objects from her teacher.
  • 1988- Attends the Perkin Institute for the Blind.
  • 1894- Attends Wright-Humason School for the Deaf.
  • 1896- Attends Cambridge School for the Young Ladies.
  • 1900- Attends Radcliffe College of Harvard University.
  • 1904 -Graduates from Radcliffe College of Harvard University at the age of 24 as the first deaf-blind person to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree.
  • 1909-Becomes an activist and advocates for many social causes like the women's suffrage and labor rights.
  • 1915- Helps establish Helen Keller International organization (research vision, health and nutrition).
  • 1918- Advocates for Braille to be the main writing system for the blind in the US.
  • 1920- Helps establish the American Civil Liberties Union (defend individual rights and liberties).
  • 1924- Starts journey as the spokeperson for the blind and and raises funds for the American Foundations for the Blind.
  • 1946- Travels the world to give motivational speeches and advocates for people with disabilities.
  • 1952- Receives the Gold Medal award from The National Institute of Social Sciences for her work.
  • 1964- Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom, highest civillian honor from President Lyndon B. Johnson.
  • 1965- Elects to National Women's Hall of Fame at the New York World's Fair.
  • 1968- Dies at the age of 87 in her sleep at home in Easton, Connecticut.
  • 1968- To this date, she has written 12 books and several articles.
